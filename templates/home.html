The problem

All countries in the world face challenges in devising strategies for clean power development but those developing, where energy needs are not yet satisfied, face an even greater challenge. A top priority in addressing these challenges is to characterize the uncertainty on future electricity demand and to understand the extent to which this demand can be impacted by public policy and technological development.

What is the goal of this project?

To project China’s electricity demand from the residential sector for the 2015-2050 period at a provincial level, under different demographic and socio-economic scenarios.

Why analyze China's power market?

Since 2011, China has surpassed the US in electricity consumption and became the country with the largest electricity consumption in the world. Now, the country faces a difficult tasks of developing an efficient, reliable, and clean electricity system commensurate with its economic growth.

Why this work is important

This tool will be useful in the analysis of strategies to achieve reliable, efficient, and sustainable electricity systems in China. If replicated, it can be used by other national and local governments to support their energy policies.

Main Model

The modeling framework developed is comprised of 4 models:

  • a) dynamic systems model that estimates China’s population at a provincial level under different scenarios
  • b) a regression model that projects, for each province, urbanization development as a function of economic growth
  • c) a regression model that estimates penetration of electric appliances for each household under different scenarios of income growth.
  • d) an integration model.
